Phase 1 — The 12-Day War · Jun 2025
13 Jun 2025Foreign Policy
Pakistan condemns Israeli strikes on Iran, declares neutrality
Israel strikes Iranian nuclear sites, military leadership and scientists, beginning the 12-Day War. Pakistan's Foreign Ministry condemns the strikes within hours and reaffirms non-alignment. FM Ishaq Dar states Pakistan's policy is "guided by national interests, not alignment with any country."
18–19 Jun 2025Domestic
Munir lunches with Trump at the White House
Field Marshal Asim Munir visits Washington for five days and is hosted by US President Trump for a 1 pm lunch in the Cabinet Room — a first for a US president to host Pakistan's army chief without senior civilian officials present. Trump praises Munir for helping prevent escalation between Pakistan and India after Operation Sindoor in May 2025. Munir also meets Secretary of State Rubio and Defense Secretary Hegseth. Discussions remain a matter of speculation — analysts point to possible coverage of ISKP, India, cryptocurrency, critical minerals, or Iran. Notably, Munir publicly states Pakistan's "clear and strong" support for Iran, while also voicing support for US de-escalation efforts.
21 Jun 2025Foreign Policy
US joins the war — Pakistan publicly condemns American strikes on Iran
US bombs Fordow, Natanz and Isfahan nuclear sites. Pakistan issues a firm condemnation — a rare public rebuke of Washington — while keeping diplomatic channels open with both sides simultaneously.
Phase 2 — Saudi pact & positioning · Sep–Dec 2025
Sep 2025Domestic
Pakistan signs Strategic Mutual Defence Agreement with Saudi Arabia
Islamabad and Riyadh sign a pact declaring that aggression against one party is aggression against both, though without automatic military intervention. Munir is seen as its driving force. The agreement embeds Pakistan within Gulf security architecture and adds a visible Sunni-aligned dimension to its balancing act with Tehran.
26 Sep 2025Domestic
Sharif & Munir meet Trump at Oval Office — Gaza plan, trade deal discussed
PM Sharif and Field Marshal Munir meet Trump at the White House. Pakistan endorses Trump's 20-point Gaza proposal. Trump calls them "incredible" and "two fantastic people" who backed the plan "100%." A US-Pakistan trade deal is on the table. Pakistan formally nominates Trump for the Nobel Peace Prize for the India–Pakistan ceasefire.
21–22 Dec 2025Domestic
Munir visits Riyadh — awarded Saudi Arabia's highest national honour
Munir meets Saudi Defence Minister Prince Khalid bin Salman in Riyadh. On King Salman's directive, he is awarded the King Abdulaziz Medal of Excellent Class — Saudi Arabia's highest honour — for strengthening bilateral defence cooperation and counterterrorism collaboration. Discussions cover regional security dynamics and evolving geopolitical challenges.
Phase 3 — Operation Epic Fury & domestic crisis · Feb–Mar 2026
28 Feb 2026Foreign Policy
US-Israel kill Khamenei — Pakistan responds within hours, offers mediation
Joint US-Israeli strikes kill Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Pakistan's MFA issues a condemnation within hours. FM Dar calls FM Araghchi to convey solidarity. Iran retaliates — partially closing the Strait of Hormuz and striking Gulf states. By 3 March, Dar addresses the Senate: "Pakistan is ready to facilitate dialogue between Washington and Tehran in Islamabad."
1 Mar 2026Sectarian
Nationwide Shia protests: 26–35 killed across Pakistan
Protests erupt across Pakistan in major cities including Karachi, Lahore, Islamabad and the contested territory of Gilgit-Baltistan as Shia communities (15–20% of Pakistan's ~250m people) react to Ali Khamenei's killing. Demonstrators burn portraits of Trump and Israeli PM Benjamin Netanyahu.

Early Mar 2026Sectarian
Munir summons Shia clerics to Rawalpindi, warns against violence
Field Marshal Munir summons Shia clerics to Rawalpindi and warns that violence inside Pakistan will not be tolerated. Munir reportedly tells the audience "if you love Iran so much, then go to Iran." The remark stokes sectarian sensitivities rather than calming them. Analysts warn of a potential Zainabiyoun Brigade recruitment revival and instability along the 900 km Iran-Pakistan border in Balochistan.
9 Mar 2026Security
Pakistan Navy launches Operation Muhafiz-ul-Bahr
As Iran partially closes the Strait of Hormuz — through which Pakistan sources roughly 90% of its oil imports — the navy deploys assets to protect Pakistani merchant vessels and maintain communication systems. Oil prices spike above $150/barrel globally, triggering fuel shortages in Karachi and Lahore.
Mar 2026Energy
Rolling blackouts hit cities — government restricts business hours
Hormuz closure disrupts oil imports. Rolling power blackouts affect Karachi, Lahore and Islamabad. The government restricts business opening hours and non-essential travel to conserve electricity. Economic analyst Khurram Husain: "The blackouts are the main problem, with knock-on effects for both industries and households."
Mar–Apr 2026Economy
Industries hit by the war
Pakistan's economy, already under a $7 billion IMF programme with inflation at 7.3% in March, faces pressure from multiple directions simultaneously.
Textiles
Largest export sector. Energy-intensive operations squeezed by soaring fuel and power tariffs. Export delays from disrupted Gulf shipping. First in line per government officials.
Cement
Energy-heavy production hit hard by rising fuel costs. Operates on narrow margins — tariff rises threaten competitiveness in an already-weak domestic market.
Remittances
54.5% of Pakistan's $38.3bn in annual remittances comes from GCC states. 5 million Pakistani workers in the Gulf face reduced demand for labour — especially construction, hospitality and retail.
Shipping & transport
Freight costs doubled. Karachi transporters report severe margin compression. Insurance costs on Gulf routes surge. $3bn in annual Pak–Iran cross-border trade disrupted.
Rice & food exports
Pakistan exports food, rice and agricultural products to UAE ($1.76bn) and Saudi Arabia ($700m) — its 5th and 7th largest global export markets. Shipping delays and cost spikes erode Gulf competitiveness.
Small & medium business
Blackouts and restricted hours force shutdowns. Lahore and Islamabad traders unable to cover wages. Medium-scale factories reducing shifts and deferred investment.
Phase 4 — Mediation & economic pressure · Late Mar–Apr 2026
3 Apr 2026Economy
Fuel prices surge over 40% in a single month — mass protests erupt
Government announces record price hikes: petrol rises to PKR 458.40/litre, diesel to PKR 520.35. Street protests erupt in Karachi, Lahore and Islamabad. IMF lowers Pakistan's 2026 growth forecast to 3.6%, down 0.8 percentage points, directly attributing the revision to the West Asia war. Consumer inflation had hit 7.3% in March.
31 Mar 2026Foreign Policy
Pakistan & China deliver 5-point peace initiative
Both countries jointly deliver a five-point initiative calling for an immediate end to all hostilities. Shuttle diplomacy intensifies — FM Dar coordinates with counterparts in Turkey and Egypt. Islamabad's mediation is driven by both diplomatic positioning and economic necessity: the Hormuz closure is directly constricting Pakistan's import-dependent economy.
1 Apr 2026Foreign Policy
US VP JD Vance visits Islamabad — Trump–Munir rapport central to Pakistan's position
JD Vance arrives in Islamabad and meets PM Shehbaz Sharif. Trump says Iran has asked for a ceasefire; Iran publicly denies it. The personal rapport between Trump and Field Marshal Munir — built at the June 2025 White House lunch — is widely cited as the foundation of Islamabad's mediator role.
7–8 Apr 2026Foreign Policy
Pakistan brokers US–Iran two-week ceasefire — Strait of Hormuz reopens
Trump announces on Truth Social that Iran has sent a 10-point proposal "mediated by Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif and Field Marshal Asim Munir" which the US considers a "workable basis to negotiate." A two-week ceasefire takes effect. Iran reopens the Strait of Hormuz to commercial shipping for the duration of the truce. Islamabad declares public holidays for 9–10 April. Over 10,000 security personnel deployed across the capital.
11–12 Apr 2026Foreign Policy
Islamabad Talks: JD Vance meets Iranian delegation — negotiations stalled
A 300-member US team (Vance, Witkoff, Kushner) meets a 70-member Iranian delegation (FM Araghchi, Speaker Ghalibaf) in Islamabad, moderated by Sharif, Munir and Dar. The US proposes an end to Iran's nuclear program, missile limits, Hormuz reopening, and restrictions on armed group support in exchange for sanctions relief. On 12 April Vance leaves without agreement. Trump subsequently announces a full naval blockade on Iran, banning all vessels to and from Iranian ports.
15–16 Apr 2026Foreign Policy
Munir flies to Tehran — carries "new message from Washington"
Munir arrives in Tehran, carrying what Pakistan's military describes as a new message from Washington. He meets FM Araghchi on Wednesday — Araghchi says he is "very pleased" to welcome Munir and affirms Iran's commitment to "promoting peace and stability." On Thursday, Munir meets Iranian Parliament Speaker Ghalibaf. Pakistan's Interior Minister Mohsin Naqvi accompanies the delegation. Iran's IRNA says the naval blockade "violated the ceasefire understanding."
17 Apr 2026Economy
Hormuz breakthrough — Trump praises "two fantastic people"
Iran announces the Strait of Hormuz will reopen to commercial shipping for the remainder of the truce. Trump posts on Truth Social: "Thank you to Pakistan and its great prime minister and field marshal, two fantastic people!" PM Sharif responds: "On behalf of the people of Pakistan, Field Marshal Syed Asim Munir, and on my behalf, I express my deep and profound appreciation for your kind and gracious words." The SBP Governor tells parliament reserves are expected to reach ~$18bn by June 2026. Real GDP grew 3.8% in H1 FY26, up from 1.8% the year prior.
21 Apr 2026Foreign Policy
Pakistan races against ceasefire expiry — Trump extends it indefinitely at Pakistan's request
As the ceasefire nears expiry, Iran publicly states it has no plans to return to talks while privately signalling a possible delegation. Trump posts on Truth Social: "Upon the request of Field Marshal Asim Munir, and Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif, of Pakistan, we have been asked to hold our Attack on the Country of Iran until such time as their leaders and representatives can come up with a unified proposal." The ceasefire is extended indefinitely. The naval blockade continues. German FM arrives in Islamabad as international pressure builds.
